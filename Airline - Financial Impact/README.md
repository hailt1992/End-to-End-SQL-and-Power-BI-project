# ✈️ Airline Pricing & Fuel Shock Dataset (2019–2026)

## 🌟 Overview
This research-grade **synthetic dataset** — calibrated against real-world benchmarks — analyzes the cascading impact of oil price shocks on the global airline industry.

It tracks how rising crude oil prices, driven by geopolitical conflicts, translated into:
- Increased airline ticket prices  
- Fuel surcharges  
- Operational disruptions  
- Financial stress across **25 global airlines**  

📅 **Time Period:** January 2019 – March 2026 (86 months)

---

## ⛽ Why This Matters
Jet fuel is the **second-largest operating cost** for airlines, accounting for **20–38% of total expenses**.

Because of this, crude oil prices act as the **single most influential external factor** affecting:
- Airfare pricing  
- Profit margins  
- Route planning  
- Airline stability  

---

## 📉 Industry Impact Captured
The dataset reflects real-world-like consequences across the aviation sector:

- ✈️ Fare increases of **9% – 25%**
- 🚫 Flight cancellations exceeding **12,000 flights/week**
- 📊 Margin compression and financial stress signals  

---

## 📊 Dataset Features
- Monthly time series data (86 months)
- 25 global airlines across multiple regions
- Fuel price indices and crude oil benchmarks
- Ticket pricing and surcharge adjustments
- Operational indicators (cancellations, capacity)
- Financial stress proxies
